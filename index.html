Helen Johnston has spent three years at Google understanding why people use the things they use. Now she's building them. She's passionate about AI that works for everyone, and tech that earns your attention by sending you back into the real world.

That's what got her second place at a global Google hackathon with Early Days, a UXR simulator, and it's what's behind Pique, an app to get people outside and into the world.

When she's not at a laptop she's behind the decks, playing high energy sets with her custom visuals. Helen also dabbles in graphic design, photography, metal welding, and has probably picked up another hobby by the time you finished reading this.
